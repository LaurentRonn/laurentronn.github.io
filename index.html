Greyboxing Moria

Exploring the halls of Moria

February 28, 2023

Hobbitses! I've been busy with building up a simple greybox level for Moria. Most of the layout is just for exploration and the purpose of this exercise is essentially to get a feel for the size and scale of what a level could look like. The main gist of it goes as follow:

1. You begin in a vast open area, maybe do some looting and BAM! Balrog appears (outta nowhere)!
2. You run through Moria fighting a bit of evil, goblins, and trolls on the way. Nasty business.
3. You arrive at the stairs and run down to a main open area. Could be a good spot to engage in some combat. More enemies appear.
4. You escape all the enemies and arrive at the bridge of Khazad-dûm.
5. You have a final confrontation with the Balrog; maybe break the bridge and escape?

Designing a level based on a structured adventure as this is quite the challenge. Not only is the process of laying out the room tedious, it is also challenging to follow the prelaid narrative. We all know how this is supposed to go: Balrog appears, fellowship runs away to bridge, Gandalf stays behind (making space...), and the fellowship escapes. This poses a problem: suppose you have picked a character, say you play as Boromir. After the bridge, you still have to escape the goblins and end the level on the other side of Moria, i.e. escape the mountain, and travel on to Lothlorien. However, say you pick Gandalf. Now you have a similar gameflow, but, once you get to the bridge, you fight off with the Balrog.
1. Then you fall.
2. Then you keep fighting him mid-air.
3. And then through fire and water.
4. From the darkest pit of Moria to its highest point, Durin's tower, above Moria upon the peak of Zirakzigil.

Now that's a challenge.
But while I figure that out, feel free to enjoy some videos and pictures below. The whole Zirakzigil thing is a bit overkill right now. :) Last but not least, Unity's built in Probuilder is very handy to quickly generate level geometry and makes it super easy to adjust and change things on the fly, so kudos to the wizards at Unity!

The lighting is awful. It looks okay on my monitor, but once in VR, the scene is very dark. I'll have to find a good lighting solution. Right now, it gets the job done.

Shadow and Flame

Introducing Durin's Bane

February 16, 2023

Well... this is fun. New website (had to refresh a few things), and of course news! For those who don't know, I'm fumbling around a little VR project codenamed "Blue Harvest" built using the Unity3D engine. Recently I started working on particles and what better exemple for particles then the one and only Durin's Bane!

Durin's Bane is a very angry Balrog, and his fire is massive. I decided to use the Unity VFX-graph to generate the particle systems, since it has the skinned mesh property. This allows you to spawn particles along the vertices of an animated mesh. The fire particles are also using a flipbook animated texture, creating a feeling of very dynamic fire and the particles fire spawn from both his head and wings.

Last but not least, the Balrog is described as a creature of shadow and flames. Using the same techniques, I added a smoke particle system to the skinnedMesh, to get that sense of fiery hell.

There is still some polish to be done! I'm thinking, sparks, heat distortion, fiery flaming sword of fire, but I'm amazed by the initial result! Check out some work in progress screenshot below!

Fly you fools!

Prototyping flight mechanics

February 16, 2023

This is the answer to the old age question: "Why don't you just fly into Mordor on the back of an Eagle?"
Well... turns out flying is hard! Not only in order to not induce motion sickness, but also in order to provide easy controls.

In order to do this, I've decided to build a flight rig. Essentially, the player rig is being pulled by the flying object (in this case the bird). The flight direction is determined by the position of the player hand, acting as the aim point. This way the player can just intuitively point and aim at a direction, and the bird flies toward that direction.

First steps in flight: getting the animation and player in position. Saddle on!

Once you get the hang of it, it's actually fun! But how do I adjust the flight speed? There is work to be done!
