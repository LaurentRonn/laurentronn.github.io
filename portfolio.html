Fire and smoke!

Introducing Durin's Bane

February 16, 2023

Well... this is fun. New website (had to refresh a few things), and of course news! For those who don't know, I'm fumbling around a little VR project codenamed "Blue Harvest" built using the Unity3D engine. Recently I started working on particles and what better exemple for particles then the one and only Durin's Bane!

Durin's Bane is a very angry Balrog, and his fire is massive. (So is his sword by the way). I decided to use the Unity VFX-graph to generate the particle systems, since it has the skinned mesh property. This allows you to spawn particles along the vertices of a animated mesh. The fire particles are also using a flipbook animated texture, creating a feeling of very dynamic fire. The fire spawns from his head and wings.

Last but not least, the Balrog is described as a creature of shadow and flames. Using the same techniques, I added a smoke particle system to the skinnedMesh, creating a sense of fiery hell.

There is still some polish to be done! I'm thinking, sparks, heat distortion, fiery flaming sword of fire, but I'm amazed by the initial result! Check out some work in progress screenshot below!

The bloom is more intense then I thought!

Integrating the Balrog in a greybox room of Moria. Feeling the heat. Now how do I fight him?

Fly you fools!

Protyping flight mechanics

February 16, 2023

This is the answer to the old age question: "Why don't you just fly into Mordor on the back of an Eagle?" Well... turns out flying is hard! Not only in order to not induce motion sickness, but also in order to provide easy controls.

In order to do this, I've decided to build a flight rig. Essentially, the player rig is being pulled by the flying object (in this case the bird). The flight direction is determined by the position of the player hand, acting as aim point. This way the player can just intuitivly point and aim at a direction, and the bird flies toward that direction.

First steps in flight: getting the animation and player in position. Saddle on!

Once you get the hang of it, it's actually fun! Now how do I adjust the flight speed?
